Bubble Tea (Boba)

by Aisyah Dems


Description

Iced tea with tapioca pearls that are sucked from a large straw are perhaps Taiwanâ€™s most famous culinary export. These bouncy, chewy balls are fun to munch on, much like chewing gum. The teas may be milky or not, sweetened or not, and flavored with any number of additional powders or infusions, like mango. My favorite style is this classic black tea with milk, and sweetened.


Ingredients

Number of servings: 2

  • 1 cup of tapioca pearls
  • 4 cups of freshly brewed strong black tea
  • 1 tablespoon of brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • Ice cubes
  • 1/2 cup of whole milk

Instruction

  1. Boil 1 cup of tapioca pearls in a hot water until the pearls fully reconstituted and softened.
  2. Drain the pearls.
  3. Mix the brown sugar with a bit of water.
  4. Soak the pearls into the mixture and mix evenly.
  5. Make the tea of your choice (light/medium/strong), add the sugar and stir to dissolve completely.
  6. Let cool and then refrigerate until completely chilled, about 2 hours, before serving.
  7. Place the tapioca pearls at the bottom of 2 cups.
  8. Use a cocktail shaker to shake together the ice, milk, and tea and strain into each cup and serve.
